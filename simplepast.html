Simple Past Tense

Introduction

The Simple Past Tense is used to describe:

  • Completed actions in the past (with a specific or implied time).
  • Past habits or states (no longer true).
  • Sequences of past events.

Definition

This tense focuses on actions that started and finished entirely in the past.

Formulas

Sentence Type Formula Examples
(+) Positive Subject + V2 (Past Simple)
  • She visited Paris last year.
  • They played soccer yesterday.
(-) Negative Subject + did + NOT + V1
  • He did not (didn’t) like the movie..
  • We did not (didn’t) go to the party.
(?) Question Did + Subject + V1?
  • Did you call him?
  • Did it rain yesterday?

Notes:

  • V2 = Past Simple form (regular: +-ed / irregular: unique forms).
  • "Did" is used for all subjects (I/you/he/they etc.).
  • Common Time Signal Words: yesterday, last night/week/month/year, ago (2 days ago), earlier today, the day before yesterday

Example Sentences

Usage Examples
Completed Past Actions
  • I finished my homework an hour ago.
  • She bought a new car last week.
Past Habits/States
  • He played tennis every Sunday (when he was young).
  • lived in Tokyo for 5 years.
Sequential Past Events
  • She woke up, brushed her teeth, and left for work.
